ET Resolution Flow

This proposal outlines a customer-led resolution flow for the ET team, standardizing how agents engage from first contact through to resolution. Every conversation is guided by the customer's needs before any solution is offered or any review update is requested.

The Resolution Flow, Step by Step

1

Ask first. Let the customer lead.

Brand

"I really appreciate you sharing your experience with us, and I would love to make this right. Is there anything specific that would help improve or resolve things for you?"

CCB

"Thank you for taking the time to share what happened. My goal is to make sure your concern is fully addressed, so I just want to ask, what do you feel would help resolve or improve your experience?"

⏳
Wait for customer response. Two paths below.

Path A: Customer knows what they want

Customer states their preferred resolution such as a refund, redo, reschedule, or provider switch.

Proceed to Step 2

Path B: Customer is not sure

Agent steps in with options tailored to the root cause of the negative review.

"I completely understand, and based on what happened, I think a [redo / reschedule / provider switch / credit] might be the best way to make this right. Would any of those work for you?"

Wait for acceptance, then proceed to Step 2

✅
Customer accepts the resolution.
2

Confirm you are working on it and check if this resolves their concern.

Brand

"Perfect, I will get that taken care of for you right away. I just want to check in, does knowing we are handling [resolution] help resolve your concern or improve your experience with us?"

CCB

"Great, I am coordinating this with [Brand] now and will follow up once confirmed. Before I do, I just want to make sure, does this resolution address what you were hoping for? Does it improve how you feel about the experience?"

✅
Customer confirms yes.
3

Soft review ask. Only after they confirm the experience improved.

Brand — Suggested Scripts (choose one)

1. "Really glad we could get that sorted for you. If that solution made a difference, I hope it gets to show in your review. It honestly goes a long way for me and the team."

2. "Good to hear that worked out. If the outcome comes through in your review at some point, that would really mean a lot. It directly impacts my performance and the team takes it seriously."

3. "Glad we landed on something that worked for you. If how things turned out ever reflects in your review, that kind of update carries real weight for me and the work we do here."

4. "Happy to hear that. If that improvement gets to show up in your review, it makes a real difference for my performance and for how the team is measured."

5. "Glad that came through for you. If you feel good about how things ended up, having that reflected in your review would genuinely help me out a lot."

CCB — Suggested Scripts (choose one)

1. "Really glad the solution worked out for you. If that positive outcome ever gets to show in your review, it carries real weight for the work I do here at the Consumer Complaints Bureau."

2. "Good to hear we got there. If how things ended up ever reflects in your review, that directly supports the work I put into cases like yours and means a lot on my end."

3. "Glad we could make that work for you. A review that reflects how things turned out now would honestly make a real difference for my performance here and shows that the process works."

4. "Happy to hear that landed well. If that improvement shows up in your review, it goes a long way in showing that what we do here actually moves the needle for people."

5. "Glad the solution came through. If you ever feel like the outcome deserves to be reflected in your review, I want you to know that would mean a lot to me and directly supports the work I do here."

↓
Customer response. Final branch.

Customer agrees to update

"That means so much, thank you! I will guide you through the steps if you need any help, and once it is updated, I will finalize everything and close this out."

Tag: Flip / Remove

Customer declines or no response

"No problem at all, the resolution still stands and I will make sure it is processed. I hope you have a wonderful rest of your day!"

Tag: Declined / No Response

Non-negotiable rule

The resolution is never conditional on the review update. Agents process the resolution regardless of whether the customer agrees to update or remove their review. The review ask is always soft, always last, and always framed around the customer's own experience. It is never a requirement or an exchange.
